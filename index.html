      General Advice

      Muscle Groups

      Learn about the major muscle groups and their functions

      Chest

      The chest muscles (pectorals) are responsible for movements like pushing and bringing your arms across your body.

      View Chest Exercises

      Back

      The back muscles (latissimus dorsi, trapezius, etc.) are responsible for pulling movements and maintaining posture.

      View Back Exercises

      Shoulders

      The deltoids allow arm rotation and lifting. Strong shoulders are crucial for upper body strength.

      View Shoulder Exercises

      Arms

      Biceps (front) and triceps (back) work together to flex and extend the elbow joint.

      View Arm Exercises

      Legs

      Quadriceps (front), hamstrings (back), and calves work together for walking, running, and jumping.

      View Leg Exercises

      Core

      Abdominals and obliques stabilize the spine and support posture and movement.

      View Core Exercises

      Exercises

      Dynamic Stretching

      Arm circles, leg swings, and torso twists to increase range of motion.

      Light Cardio

      5-10 minutes of jogging, jumping jacks, or cycling to elevate heart rate.

      Bodyweight Movements

      Bodyweight squats, lunges, and push-ups to activate muscles.

      Pre & Post Workout Nutrition

      Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before)

      Focus on easily digestible carbs and some protein:

      • Banana with peanut butter
      • Oatmeal with berries
      • Whole grain toast with eggs

      Post-Workout (within 30-60 minutes)

      Focus on protein and carbs to replenish glycogen and repair muscles:

      • Protein shake with banana
      • Grilled chicken with rice and vegetables
      • Greek yogurt with granola
